# Getting Started with BPM Tap Tempo

Welcome to BPM Tap Tempo! This guide will help you get started with finding the tempo of any song.

## What is BPM?

BPM stands for Beats Per Minute. It's the standard measurement for tempo in music. Whether you're a DJ mixing tracks, a musician practicing, or a producer creating beats, knowing the BPM is essential.

## How to Use the Tool

1. **Play your music** - Start the song you want to analyze
2. **Tap along** - Click or tap the TAP button in rhythm with the beat
3. **Read the result** - After a few taps, you'll see the calculated BPM

## Tips for Accuracy

- **Tap consistently** - Try to tap on the same beat each time
- **Use more taps** - The more taps, the more accurate the result
- **Focus on the kick** - The bass drum usually marks the main beat
- **Reset if needed** - If you lose the rhythm, hit Reset and start over

## Understanding Tempo Categories

- **Largo (40-60 BPM)** - Very slow, often used in ballads
- **Andante (80-100 BPM)** - Walking pace, comfortable tempo
- **Allegro (120-140 BPM)** - Fast and bright, common in pop music
- **Presto (180+ BPM)** - Very fast, found in drum and bass

## Common BPM Ranges by Genre

- Hip Hop: 85-115 BPM
- House: 120-130 BPM
- Techno: 130-150 BPM
- Drum & Bass: 160-180 BPM
- Pop: 100-130 BPM

Start tapping and discover the tempo of your favorite songs!
